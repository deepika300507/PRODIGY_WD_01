Whale

The blue whale (Balaenoptera musculus) is a marine mammal and a baleen whale.The blue whale is usually solitary, but can be found in pairs. When productivity is high enough, blue whales can be seen in gatherings of more than 50 individuals

Blue whale typically swim at 2–8 kilometers per hour (1.2–5.0 mph) but may swim faster at 32–36 kilometers per hour (20–22 mph) during encounters with boats, predators or other individuals.

Blue whales live around 80–90 years or more.Each year, a light and dark layer of wax is laid corresponding with fasting during migration and feeding time.

The oldest blue whale found was determined, using this method, to be 110 years old.

The idea that a blue whale senses its impending death and performs one final, intentional dive to the deepest part of the ocean is a popular internet narrative, but it is not supported by scientific evidence.
